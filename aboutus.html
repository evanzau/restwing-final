Our Story

Built out of
frustration.

RestWing didn't start in a lab. It started in the backseat of a car on a long drive from Vegas to LA — and a simple question: why doesn't anything work?

The Origin

A problem everyone has.
A solution nobody built.

Two years ago, RestWing founder Aaditya Gulati was a passenger on a drive from Las Vegas to Los Angeles. He tried the window, the armrest, even his backpack — nothing worked. He couldn't get comfortable, and he noticed his friends struggled with the exact same thing every single trip.

That frustration became a question, and that question became RestWing — a clip-on ergonomic headrest attachment that provides the lateral support no travel pillow has ever offered.

"My life has always been shaped by entrepreneurship. Ever since I was a kid watching Shark Tank, I've looked at everything around me as a potential idea waiting to be built." — Aaditya Gulati, Founder

"What makes RestWing different is that it's totally bendable — you can shape it exactly how you want instead of being stuck with one position."

Aaditya Gulati — ANTrepreneur Center Interview, 2025

Engineering

Engineered with purpose.
Built to last.

RestWing is built from a 3D-printed clamp mechanism, an aluminum wire frame, and memory foam contact wings. Every material was chosen after extensive testing — finding the perfect balance where the device is flexible enough to bend into any position, yet strong enough to hold its shape throughout the ride.

The team partnered with Invento Design Engineering at UCI's Beall Applied Innovation center to bring the prototype to manufacturing readiness — working with an Orange County-based manufacturer to prepare for launch.
